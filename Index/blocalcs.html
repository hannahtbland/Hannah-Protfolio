BLOCAL

BLOCAL is an application to make it easier for users to shop and eat local in their area with just a
couple of clicks. We wanted to design a unique feature that current resurants don't have.

Role

UX Designer

Team

Jen Christner
Alex Polen

Timeline

3 weeks

Tools

Figma, Miro,
Invision, Googl Drive

Deliverables

One User Path High Fidelity Prototype

Background

For this project we take you through our design story of how we got to our final design of BLOCAL. BLOCAL started out as a combination of ideas when our team was prompted to design a new app that we thought was important to us. After many discussions and sticky notes later we found that supporting our community was something we wanted help and focus on. With this we all found it was hard sometimes finding local restaurants especially if you were hungry. That we wanted to design something different to help users.

Research

Once we had an idea of what we wanted to design we needed to know who the users were. We began by creating a proto persona which would set the structure of our interviews.
Our proto persona Margot helped us develop our question and our initial research problem statement that we wanted to know more about to support it.

Research Problem Statement

It can be hard to break out of your comfort zone in your home town or city. We aim to help people find new and exciting experiences that break away from the corporate chains.

Findings

After conducting our interviews and getting the results from posting our survey we were able to understand what users needed and what their path was to find locally owned restaurants.

From our notes in our interviews we put them in sticky notes of the main takeaways for each user interview.

After we placed all our notes on sticky notes we started to group them together based on similarity and what users had in common. Which we then divided up into an affinity diagram to break down and understand what we might need to focus on in our design.

After compiling all our rnotes and building our affinity diagram we were able to understand was user we were designing BLOCAL for Abby our user persona.

Definition

With Abby we were able to start the process of understanding our problem that we wanted to solve. We started with doing a competitor analysis to see what competitors that were similar to our idea had success in and where it falls short. We also included ones that were indirect to our idea and did the same thing.
From the competitor analysis we took the user insights that stood out to us the most and focused on those to help build on to get us to our problem statement.

User Insights

  • Visuals important
  • Impatient, too many options
  • Want information
  • Important to support locally owned restaurants
  • Comfort zone
  • Sometimes disappointed

After the research and understanding the users and many attempts to achieve a problem statement we believed best described what we wanted to solve we got our PROBLEM STATEMENT:

BLOCAL is designed to make restaurant goers feel confident and excited in their locally owned restaurant choices. We observed when users are hungry they make impromptu decisions because information on current apps is cluttered and overwhelming. How might we streamline search information into a simple and visually appealing layout so restaurant goers successfully choose a restaurant and return to the app to leave a review?

Ideation

Once we had our problem statement we finally could start our ideation & our design process. We first began by brainstorming ideas of what we wanted BLOCAL to have by using the I like, I wish, What if method. Once we had all our ideas on sticky notes we conducted a dot voting method not just with us but with potential users.

The ideas that had dots on them we moved them to the side and started to prioritize them as a group and discuss which ones were idealistic and what would actually be beneficial to our users with still be different. That we came up with a feature that we hadn't seen before and was something that would be impactful to the client

On The Fly

This feature allows users who are on the fly to be able to get a locally owned restaurant suggested to them whether it was a saved preference or something completely random in a matter of seconds.

Once we had our feature that we wanted to focus on we started ideating user scenarios and storyboarding when a user would want to use this feature. Which helped us build out our user flow for Abby and other users. We did build out multiple user flows specific to the on the fly feature for users who had an account with BLOCAL or didn't as we wanted users to still be able to use the app without having an account.

Prototype

Once we had our user flows defined and finalized we started sketching our low fidelity prototypes. Each team member sketched their own prototype. We compared our designs and we all conducted user testings with our prototypes. The benefits of each of us doing user testing for each of our individual designs and user flow was to see what ones we wanted to take from each other and get different feedback to see what users had an easier time using.

Finding

Though our designs were very simillar to each other we gained great feed back that we knew what we need to enhance in our high fidelity prototype

Some of the user insights we got after our user testing was:

  • Couldn’t identify purpose of On the Fly
  • Too much text on coaching screens
  • On the Fly buttons not recognizable
  • Not clear if overriding saved preferences in On the Fly for account holder
  • Like vs. bookmark unclear

Outcome

From our initial user testing and additional ones after having a higher fidelity prototype. Users found our on the fly button more recognizable and easier to use. Users liked that they could still enter in their preference if they didn't have an account or go straight into giving them a random suggestion. That they thought was fun and unique took hours to find a restaurant out.

Conclusion

Hardest part was coming up with something different from existing apps and services out there that is like ours. Then showing & explaining what that new feature does as it is something new that users haven't experienced before.

However, for the future & possible enhancements that I would like to see and as a team we would like to expand outside of just restaurants. Gather more real-world feedback on the user's experience with the app. Add map/directions feature. Add map search feature. Add a Plan Your Night feature - user provides location and radius and randomizer provides food, activities, etc.
